1. Warm the **coconut oil** in a large pot over medium heat. Add the **onion** with a pinch of salt and cook for 5–6 minutes until soft and translucent.
2. Stir in the **garlic**, **ginger**, **cumin**, **turmeric** and **garam masala**. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add the **rinsed lentils**, **chopped tomatoes**, **coconut milk** and **vegetable stock**. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer.
4. Simmer uncovered for 22–25 minutes, stirring now and then, until the lentils are soft and the dahl has thickened.
5. Fold through the **spinach** until just wilted. Stir in the **lime juice** and season to taste.
6. Serve scattered with **fresh coriander**, with rice or flatbread alongside.

## Notes

- For a lighter version, swap full-fat coconut milk for the light variety — it noticeably lowers the saturated fat.
- The dahl thickens as it cools; loosen with a splash of water when reheating.
